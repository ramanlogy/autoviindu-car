Policy Types

Third-Party vs Comprehensive

Nepal law requires at minimum third-party insurance. Here is what each policy covers — and what it does not.

Third-Party
Third-Party Insurance
Mandatory by law

Covers damage or injury you cause to other people, their vehicles, or property. Quick to arrange, with regulated premium rates set by Beema Samiti.

What it covers
Legal liability for third-party death or injury
Third-party property damage
Legal costs and court fees if applicable
What it does NOT cover
Your own vehicle damage
Your own injuries
Theft of your vehicle
Fire or natural calamity damage to your vehicle
Comprehensive
Comprehensive Insurance
Recommended

Everything in third-party, plus full protection for your own vehicle against accidents, theft, fire, and Nepal's natural calamities — floods, earthquakes, landslides.

Everything in Third-Party, plus
Own vehicle damage (accidents, collisions)
Theft and burglary
Fire damage
Natural calamities — floods, earthquakes, landslides
Riots, strikes, and malicious acts
EV battery and charging equipment damage
Damage during transit (if applicable)
Does NOT cover
General wear and tear or mechanical breakdown
Driving under the influence (DUI)
Driving outside declared geographical area
Consequential losses (loss of earnings)

No Claim Bonus

Drive Clean,
Pay Less

No Claim Bonus (NCB) rewards you with a discount on next year's premium for every claim-free year. It belongs to you — not the vehicle — so you can take it to a new car.

Smart tip
For minor damages, consider paying out-of-pocket. One claim resets your entire NCB to 0%. Check whether your repair cost is less than the NCB discount you would lose.
Accumulates year after year
The longer you stay claim-free, the higher your discount becomes at each renewal.
Fully transferable
NCB belongs to you, not the vehicle. Change your car and your NCB comes with you.
One claim resets to zero
Any claim — however small — wipes your NCB completely. You restart from 0% next year.
Only on own damage premium
NCB does not apply to the third-party portion of your premium — only own damage.
Exclusions

What is Not Covered

Knowing these before you drive prevents unpleasant surprises at claim time. These apply to both third-party and comprehensive policies.

Drunk Driving
Any accident while under influence of alcohol or drugs — claim rejected.
No Valid License
Driver holds no valid or expired license at the time of accident.
Mechanical Breakdown
Faults arising from internal mechanical or electrical failure — not accident related.
Normal Wear & Tear
Gradual deterioration of parts over time is not a covered peril.
Consequential Damage
Losses that occur as an indirect result of the main incident.
Commercial Use
Private vehicles used for hire or delivery (taxi, Pathao, courier) are not covered.
Outside Declared Area
Using the vehicle outside the permitted geographical area may void your claim.
Depreciation on Parts
Deducted on replaced parts unless you have the Zero Depreciation add-on active.
War / Terrorism
Damages caused by war, nuclear events, or terrorist activities are excluded.

EV components need specialist cover
Battery, charging equipment, and electrical systems require dedicated coverage. Battery depreciation is also calculated differently from regular vehicle depreciation.

Option 2 — Fresh Policy in Your Name

Vehicle inspection may be required depending on age
IDV based on current market value minus applicable depreciation
Battery State of Health (SoH) assessment required for used EVs

Avoid underinsuring your vehicle
Do not declare a very low IDV just to reduce your premium. In the event of total loss or theft, your settlement is based strictly on the declared IDV. Always reflect the true current market value.

FAQs

Common Questions

Straight answers to the questions we get asked most.

Is car insurance mandatory in Nepal?
Yes. Third-party insurance is mandatory under the Motor Vehicles Act. Driving without it means an on-spot fine and possible vehicle impoundment. Comprehensive insurance is optional but strongly recommended.
What is the difference between third-party and comprehensive?
Third-party covers damage you cause to others — their vehicle or injury. Your own vehicle is not covered. Comprehensive covers everything in third-party plus your own vehicle against accident, theft, fire, and natural calamities.
Can I drive immediately after buying insurance?
Yes. Your policy activates immediately upon payment. You receive a digital certificate via email and SMS right away. Physical certificate is delivered within 3–5 days.
What if I miss my renewal date?
There is a grace period depending on your insurer, but you are legally uninsured during this window. If an accident occurs in the grace period, there is no coverage. Renew before expiry to avoid this risk. We send reminders 30, 15, and 7 days before expiry.
Do I lose my NCB if I make a small claim?
Yes. Any claim — however small — resets your NCB completely to 0% for the next year. For minor repairs, consider paying out of pocket. Calculate whether your NCB discount is worth more than the repair cost.
Can I claim insurance if I was at fault in the accident?
Yes, if you have comprehensive insurance. Your own vehicle damage is covered regardless of fault. However, standard exclusions still apply — drunk driving, no valid license, and so on.
What documents are needed to buy insurance?
Vehicle Registration Certificate (Bluebook), valid driving license, previous insurance policy copy (for renewal or NCB transfer), citizenship or passport, and address proof. For renewals we keep your documents on file.
Can I transfer my insurance to a new car?
The policy itself is vehicle-specific and cannot be transferred to a different car. However, your NCB belongs to you personally and can be applied to a new car's insurance premium.
How long does claim settlement take?
Minor damage: 10–15 days. Major damage: 15–25 days. Total loss: 25–40 days. Theft: 60–90 days after police investigation is completed.
What happens if my car is stolen?
For comprehensive policies: file an FIR immediately, inform your insurer within 24 hours, and submit the original keys and RC book. After the investigation period (60–90 days), the insurer pays the declared IDV minus the policy deductible. Third-party does not cover theft.
Does insurance cover Nepal's earthquakes and floods?
Yes — for comprehensive policies. Natural calamities including earthquakes, floods, and landslides are covered as standard under comprehensive insurance in Nepal. Third-party does not cover your own vehicle damage from any cause.
Can I get insurance for a very old car (15+ years)?
Comprehensive insurance is difficult for vehicles over 10 years old. Some specialized insurers may offer coverage after a vehicle inspection. Third-party insurance is always available — it is mandatory regardless of vehicle age.
