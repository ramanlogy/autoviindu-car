No Claim Bonus (NCB) rewards you with a discount on next year's own-damage premium for every claim-free year. It belongs to you — not the vehicle — so it follows you to a new car.

Smart tip

What is Not Covered

Knowing these before you drive prevents surprises at claim time.

Drunk Driving
Any accident under influence of alcohol or drugs — claim rejected outright.
No Valid Licence
Driver holds no valid or expired licence at time of accident — full rejection.
Mechanical Breakdown
Faults from internal mechanical or electrical failure — not accident related.
Normal Wear & Tear
Gradual deterioration of parts over time is not a covered peril.
Consequential Damage
Losses that occur as an indirect result of the main incident.
Commercial Use
Private vehicles used for hire or delivery (taxi, Pathao, courier) are not covered.
Outside Declared Area
Using the vehicle outside the permitted geographical area may void your claim.
Depreciation on Parts
Deducted on replaced parts unless Zero Depreciation add-on is active.
War / Terrorism
Damages caused by war, nuclear events, or terrorist activities.

Insurance FAQs

Common Insurance Questions

Straight answers to the questions we get asked most.

Is car insurance mandatory in Nepal?
Yes. Third-party insurance is mandatory under the Motor Vehicles Act. Driving without it means an on-spot fine and possible vehicle impoundment. Comprehensive is optional but strongly recommended.
Can I drive immediately after buying insurance?
Yes. Your policy activates immediately upon payment. You receive a digital certificate via email and SMS right away. Physical certificate delivered within 3–5 days.
What if I miss my renewal date?
There is a grace period, but you are legally uninsured during this window. If an accident occurs in the grace period, there is no coverage. We send reminders 30, 15, and 7 days before expiry to help you avoid this.
Do I lose my NCB if I make a small claim?
Yes. Any claim — however small — resets your NCB to 0% for the next year. For minor repairs, consider paying out of pocket and preserving your NCB discount.
Does insurance cover Nepal's earthquakes and floods?
Yes — for comprehensive policies. Natural calamities including earthquakes, floods, and landslides are covered as standard. Third-party does not cover your own vehicle from any cause.
What documents are needed to buy insurance?
Vehicle Registration Certificate (Bluebook), valid driving licence, previous insurance policy copy (for renewal or NCB transfer), citizenship or passport, and address proof.
Can I claim if I was at fault in the accident?
Yes, with comprehensive insurance. Your own vehicle damage is covered regardless of fault. Standard exclusions still apply — drunk driving, no valid licence, etc.
What happens if my car is stolen?
For comprehensive: file an FIR immediately, inform your insurer within 24 hours, submit original keys and RC book. After the 60–90 day investigation, insurer pays the declared IDV minus deductible. Third-party does not cover theft.
Can I get insurance for a very old car (15+ years)?
Comprehensive is difficult for vehicles over 10 years old. Some specialised insurers may offer cover after vehicle inspection. Third-party is always available — mandatory regardless of vehicle age.
Can I cancel my insurance mid-year?
Yes, but: refund = premium paid minus expired period premium minus cancellation charges. Your NCB for that year is also lost. Usually not beneficial unless vehicle is sold or declared total loss.

Financing FAQs

Common Financing Questions

Everything you need to know before applying for a vehicle loan in Nepal.

How much loan can I get for a new vehicle?
As per current NRB directives, lenders can finance up to 60% of the vehicle's value. You need to arrange at least 40% as a down payment for new vehicles.
Do EVs get better loan terms?
Yes. EVs attract lower interest rates due to the government's green loan incentives and customs duty relief. Class A banks offer EV loans up to 10 years — longer than the 7-year maximum for ICE vehicles.
What is the difference between fixed and variable rates?
A fixed rate stays the same throughout your loan tenure — your EMI never changes. A variable rate fluctuates based on the lender's base rate, meaning your EMI can go up or down over time.
What is Hire Purchase and how is it different from a bank loan?
In a Hire Purchase arrangement, the lender technically owns the vehicle while you are making payments. Full ownership transfers to you on the final installment. It is offered by NRB-licensed companies rather than commercial banks, and is one of the primary options for used vehicle financing.
Can I get a loan for a used vehicle?
Yes, but options are more limited. Used vehicle financing is primarily offered by Class A Commercial Banks and NRB-licensed Hire Purchase Companies. Class B and C banks do not currently offer used vehicle loans.
Is a vehicle valuation required for used vehicle loans?
Yes. A mandatory valuation report is required before loan disbursement. For used EVs, a Battery State of Health (SoH) assessment is also required to assess battery condition and remaining capacity.
Is bluebook transfer mandatory?
Absolutely. Bluebook transfer to the buyer's name is mandatory before any loan is disbursed for a used vehicle. Lenders will not proceed without this.
Can loan terms change after I apply?
Yes. All rates and terms are subject to revision based on Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB) directives and government policies. Always confirm the latest terms directly with your chosen institution before finalising.
