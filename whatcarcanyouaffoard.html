How it works
The 25% Rule
Keep total car costs under 25% of your take-home pay. Most relaxed, most savings.
The 35% Rule
All car expenses — loan, fuel, insurance, maintenance — under 35% of gross income. Standard advice worldwide.
The 20/4/10 Rule
Put 20% down, borrow for max 4 years, total monthly car costs under 10% of gross. Strictest rule for maximum financial health.
True monthly cost
EMI isn't the full picture. We add estimated fuel (1,000 km/month at Rs. 175/L), insurance, and routine servicing.
Buy vs lease
Buying builds equity — after 5 years it's yours. In Nepal, banks typically offer purchase loans, not leases. Certified pre-owned cars reduce upfront depreciation.
This is a guide only. Your actual EMI, interest rate, and tax depend on your lender and income. Always confirm with your bank before purchasing.
