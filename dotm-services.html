FAQs

Common Questions

Clear answers to what people ask most before requesting a service.

Can I change my vehicle's registration address through you?
Yes. If you have relocated or need to update your registered address in the bluebook, we handle the full process with DOTM including all required documentation.
How long does a driving license take in Nepal?
The full process typically takes 30–90 days depending on appointment availability, exam performance, and smart license printing timelines. We help expedite each stage through proactive booking and document readiness.
What is hypothecation entry and removal?
Hypothecation entry is when a bank's lien is recorded in your bluebook against a vehicle loan. Hypothecation removal is when the loan is cleared and the bank's name is removed. We assist with both, in coordination with the bank and DOTM.
What is an IDP and can you process it?
An International Driving Permit (IDP) allows you to drive in foreign countries and is issued alongside your Nepali driving license. We process IDP applications for Nepali citizens planning to drive abroad. An IDP supplements — it does not replace — your national license.
Do you offer corporate fleet registration?
Yes. For businesses, companies, and organisations with multiple vehicles, we provide bulk and corporate fleet registration support — handling multiple vehicles simultaneously to save time and effort.
Do you manage DOTM appointments on my behalf?
Yes. Appointment management is a core part of our service. We monitor availability, book slots proactively, and reschedule if needed — ensuring no delays from missed or unavailable appointments.
Is the green sticker required?
Yes. The emission certificate (green sticker) confirms your vehicle meets Nepal government emission standards. It is required during vehicle renewal and is actively enforced in Kathmandu Valley. We coordinate the test and issuance for you.
Can you process multiple vehicles at once?
Yes. We offer bulk vehicle processing for organisations needing to register, renew, or transfer multiple vehicles simultaneously. This is suitable for companies, government agencies, NGOs, and transport operators.
